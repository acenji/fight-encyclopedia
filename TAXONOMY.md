# Fight Encyclopedia Taxonomy

## The 7-Level Hierarchy

Fight Encyclopedia uses a **biological-style classification system** to organize every known fighting technique. Just as biology classifies life into Kingdom > Phylum > Class > Order > Family > Genus > Species, we classify techniques into:

```
Level 1: Class        (broadest — 9 total)
Level 2: Group        (58 total)
Level 3: Family       (220 total)
Level 4: SubFamily    (480 total)
Level 5: Genus        (632 total)
Level 6: Species      (102 total)
Level 7: Variety      (114 total)
```

### Why This Structure?

Traditional martial arts resources organize techniques by art (BJJ techniques, Judo techniques, etc.). This creates fragmentation — the same armbar exists in BJJ, Judo, Sambo, and MMA but is documented separately in each.

Our taxonomy organizes by **biomechanical function** — what the technique does, not which art claims it. This reveals connections:
- A Judo *juji-gatame* and a BJJ *armbar* are the same technique
- A wrestling *double leg* and a Judo *morote-gari* share mechanics
- A Muay Thai *teep* and a Karate *mae-geri* have common biomechanics

### Example Path

```
Submission (Class)
 └── Choke & Strangle Lock (Group)
      └── Leg Choke (Family)
           └── Triangular Strangle (SubFamily)
                └── Triangle Choke (Genus)
                     └── Triangle From Closed Guard (Species)
                          └── Standard Triangle (Variety)
```

This path tells you: the Standard Triangle is a variety of Triangle from Closed Guard, which is a species of Triangle Choke, which belongs to the Triangular Strangle subfamily of Leg Chokes, within the broader Choke & Strangle Lock group of Submissions.

### Cross-Art Classification

The same genus can contain species from different martial arts:

```
Triangle Choke (Genus)
 ├── Triangle From Closed Guard (Species) — BJJ
 ├── Mounted Triangle (Species) — BJJ/MMA
 ├── Side Triangle (Species) — BJJ
 ├── Flying Triangle (Species) — BJJ/MMA
 └── Sankaku-jime (Species) — Judo
```

All are the same mechanical technique, but applied from different positions and traditions.

### Japanese Names

Every technique has a Japanese name following the Rosetta Stone protocol:
- **Traditional** (伝統的): Techniques with established Japanese names (e.g., 三角絞め for Triangle Choke)
- **Gairaigo** (外来語): Modern techniques using katakana transliteration (e.g., ニースライスパス for Knee Slice Pass)
- **Hybrid** (混合): Mix of kanji concepts + katakana loanwords

This reflects how the Japanese BJJ and martial arts community actually names techniques.
